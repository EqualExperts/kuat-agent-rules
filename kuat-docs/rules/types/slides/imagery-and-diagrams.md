# Slides — Imagery and Diagrams

Photography placement, diagrams, and icons on slides.

## Photography

Slide-specific placement and treatment. All EE branded photography is monochrome (black and white).

- **Always black-and-white.** Convert colour photos before placement.
- **Real EE people, real client environments** — use the EE Image Library first. Stock photography is a last resort and must be desaturated to match treatment.
- **Subjects working, not posing.** Hands on keyboards, sticky notes on walls, conversation in progress. Avoid corporate handshake stock.
- **Compositional space for overlays.** Choose photos with a quieter region where a bracket frame, translucent panel, or title block will sit.

### Do

- Use a single full-bleed image with a bracket frame for section dividers and the title slide.
- Use the translucent-white-panel-over-photo treatment when the background photo is busy but you need readable text.
- Crop to focus on people and action, not backgrounds.

### Don't

- Do not use colour photography, even client photos — convert to B&W.
- Do not use AI-generated photos of people. AI-generated abstract or conceptual imagery is acceptable if marked as such and only on conceptual slides (e.g., AI service explainer).
- Do not place text directly on a high-contrast area of a photo without a backing panel.
- Do not use photos of identifiable third parties without licence/consent.

## Diagrams

- Line-based, monochromatic, EE Blue or Tech Blue strokes on white.
- Sparse labels, generous whitespace.
- Use the EE icon set for nodes; do not mix icon styles.
- Reuse template diagram patterns where they fit: pyramid (experience distribution), dotted world map (reach), node-and-edge (architecture), flow (process).

### Do

- Annotate diagrams with the takeaway in the slide title, not inside the diagram.
- Keep stroke weight consistent across all diagram elements.

### Don't

- Do not import vendor diagrams (AWS, GCP, etc.) without restyling — they break the visual system. Either redraw in EE style or place inside a card with a "source: AWS" caption.
- Do not use 3D, isometric, or skeuomorphic styles.
- Do not colour-code more than three categories in a single diagram.

---

## Icons

- Use the EE line-icon set at consistent stroke weight and size.
- Place icons on EE Blue squares with white strokes for card headers (see *Card grid* in [layouts.md](./layouts.md)).
- Place icons inline at body-text height when used within a paragraph for emphasis.
