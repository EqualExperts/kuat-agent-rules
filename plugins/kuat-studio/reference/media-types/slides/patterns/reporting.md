# Slides — Reporting Scenario

**Job-to-be-done:** Communicate findings, status, or analysis to a stakeholder who will read offline. Internal team reviews, client status reports, research findings, audits.

**Typical deck shape:** Title → executive summary → method → findings (one per slide) → recommendations → appendix. ~20–60 slides depending on depth.

**Density:** Higher — each slide must stand alone because the reader is offline. Body copy carries the argument, not the presenter.

---

## Do

- Open with a one-slide executive summary: situation, key findings (3–5 bullets), recommendation. Make this slide work as the standalone takeaway.
- One finding per slide, with the finding as the title, supporting evidence in the body, and implication in a bolded closer.
- Use tables and charts liberally — this is the scenario they earn their place in.
- Include a "how we got to this view" method slide early — readers will trust the findings more.
- Put detail (raw data, full transcripts, methodology specifics) in an appendix section behind a clearly labelled divider.

---

## Don't

- Do not use sales-deck testimonial or customer-logo slides in a report — wrong register.
- Do not present hedged conclusions ("findings may suggest…") if the data supports a clearer claim. State the claim, then qualify if needed.
- Do not bury the recommendation in the appendix — it goes near the top, immediately after the executive summary.
