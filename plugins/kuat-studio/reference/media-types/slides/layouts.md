# Slides — Layouts

Canonical slide layouts. Pick the one that matches the slide's job — do not blend.

**See also:** [styling.md](./styling.md), [imagery-and-diagrams.md](./imagery-and-diagrams.md).

> **The master template is the source of truth for layout.** These layouts live in the genuine EE master (`assets/slides/ee-master-2026.pptx`); decks are **built from it**, so the structure is inherited, not re-described. The brand signature is the **left-side "[" bracket** — a full-height tech-blue element on the **left edge** of content/section slides. (An earlier version of this file wrongly described it as "top-left + bottom-right corner framing"; that is corrected below.)

---

## Layout catalogue

1. **Title slide** — Hero photograph (B&W), the left-side "[" bracket, blue title bar across the bottom carrying deck title + subtitle + EE logo right.
2. **Section divider** — Photograph with translucent white or blue panel overlay, the left-side "[" bracket, centred title + subtitle on the panel. Use to mark major shifts in the narrative.
3. **Content — light** — White background, left-side "[" bracket, eyebrow + title at top-left, body in left column, optional image or stat panel right.
4. **Content — dark** — Slate-900 background, same structure as light. Use for *About us*, *What we do*, key narrative beats.
5. **Split — text + image** — 60/40 text/image. Image is always B&W, always to the right unless explicitly mirrored.
6. **Stats panel** — Vertical stack of three or four blue-toned cells (EE Blue → Tech Blue → Slate-900), each pairing a metric with a one-line context. Lives in the right column alongside a narrative left column.
7. **Card grid (services / capabilities)** — 4-up or 8-up grid of icon-headed cards. Icon top, label on EE Blue strip, supporting copy in body cell. Alternate light/dark cells for rhythm.
8. **Case study slide** — Diagonal "CASE STUDY" ribbon top-right, client logo top-left, slide title beside logo, image left half, two stacked story panels right half (challenge in Tech Blue panel, outcomes in light panel).
9. **Testimonial / quote** — Large opening + closing quote marks, quote in body size, attribution below, client logo top-right. Single quote per slide, or paired side-by-side for two quotes max.
10. **Customer logo grid** — Bordered cells in 5–7 column grid, logos centred and sized to optical balance, not pixel size.
11. **Contact / closing** — Slate-900 background, headshot left, name + role + email + phone right, social row at bottom.

---

## Do

- Reuse layouts. A 60-slide deck should use 5–8 layouts, not 60.
- Match layout to content job: stats panel for outcomes, card grid for capability menus, case study for one named client engagement.

---

## Don't

- Do not invent hybrid layouts (e.g., stats panel inside a case study slide). If the content needs more than one layout's worth of treatment, split it across slides.
- Do not stack more than four items in a stats panel — readability drops sharply.
- Do not use the case-study layout for a hypothetical or composite client. The ribbon implies a real, named engagement.
