# Slides — Knowledge Sharing Scenario

**Job-to-be-done:** Teach a concept, share a finding, prompt a discussion. Typically internal (UCD network, communities of practice) or external (conference, meetup).

**Typical deck shape:** Title → why this matters → core concept → examples / demonstration → implications / what to try → questions. ~15–25 slides.

**Density:** Very sparse for live presentation. The presenter is the content; the slide is punctuation.

---

## Do

- Use the dark content slide for the key concept — it slows the audience down at the moment that matters.
- Lean into diagrams, illustrations, and live demo screenshots over text.
- Include one "things to try on Monday" slide near the end — converts attendance into action.
- Cite sources where claims are not your own — internal cred matters in this audience.

---

## Don't

- Do not use the case-study layout for hypothetical examples. Use a generic content slide and label clearly as "illustrative".
- Do not over-brand a community-of-practice deck — masterbrand-lite, single eyebrow + logo on title and closing, page-number badge throughout, that's enough.
- Do not include the commercial / sales sections — wrong context, breaks trust.
