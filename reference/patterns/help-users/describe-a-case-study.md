# Help users to describe a case study

**User goal:** Understand a real client story — the challenge, what Equal Experts did, and the outcome —
well enough to trust EE with a similar problem.

This is a **shared concept**: the principles below hold across every medium that tells case studies. Each
medium's implementation links back here for the *why*, and owns the *how* for its own format.

## Context

A case study is how EE turns experience into credibility — a named (or carefully anonymised) client story
that shows the problem, the approach, and measurable outcomes. It appears wherever EE makes its track
record concrete: in a sales deck, on a marketing page, in a capability overview. The same story is told
differently in each medium, but the goal and the marks of a good telling are shared.

## Principles

- Lead with the client's challenge framed in their terms, not EE's.
- Make outcomes concrete and measurable — headline metrics first, qualitative outcomes after.
- Establish credibility with specifics — technologies, scale, and team shape, where they add weight.
- Claim proportionately: "helped reduce", not "transformed", unless it genuinely was a transformation.
- Respect approval and confidentiality — a named case study needs client approval; anonymise only where
  required, and never to the point of meaninglessness ("a large UK retailer" with no metrics carries no
  weight).

## Implementations

This concept is implemented in the media that tell case studies:

- **Slides** — [case studies](../../media-types/slides/patterns/case-studies.md): a standalone sales
  artefact that also embeds into a larger deck.
- **Web-marketing** — *planned*: a marketing-page case study, to be added when web-marketing adopts the
  pattern model.

It has **no web-product implementation** — a case study is a sales and marketing artefact, not a
web-application page type.
